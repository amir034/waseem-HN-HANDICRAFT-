About Us

Our Story

HN Handicraft began with a simple belief: that the hands behind India's finest traditions deserve a stage as beautiful as the work they create. What started as a small curators' table at local craft fairs has grown into a platform connecting skilled makers from Kutch, Varanasi, Jaipur, Kashmir, and beyond with homes across the world.

Every product in our collection is chosen for its authenticity, quality, and the story it carries — from handwoven Kala cotton throws to meenakari enamel, Banarasi silk, and rosewood inlay. We work directly with artisan families and cooperatives, ensuring fair wages and long-term partnerships that keep endangered crafts alive.

When you shop with us, you are not just buying a product. You are preserving a legacy, supporting a livelihood, and bringing a piece of India's living heritage into your everyday life.

What We Believe

Our Philosophy

At HN Handicraft, craft is not a commodity — it is culture, identity, and care made visible. These principles guide every decision we make, from the artisan we partner with to the product that reaches your doorstep.

01

Authenticity First

We celebrate original techniques passed down through generations — block printing, zardozi embroidery, filigree, kantha stitch, and wood inlay — never mass-produced imitations.

02

Fair & Transparent

Artisans receive fair compensation for their skill and time. We believe in honest pricing, direct relationships, and complete transparency about where and how each piece is made.

03

Sustainable by Design

Natural fibres, organic dyes, locally sourced materials, and low-waste production are at the heart of traditional Indian craft. We choose partners who share this respect for the earth.

04

Craft Meets Contemporary

Heritage need not feel outdated. We collaborate with artisans to reimagine classic forms for modern homes — functional, beautiful, and rooted in tradition.

05

Every Piece Has a Story

No two handcrafted items are exactly alike. Variations in weave, patina, and finish are marks of the human hand — and we celebrate them, not hide them.

06

Community Over Commerce

Our success is measured not only in sales, but in the number of artisan families we sustain and the crafts we help keep alive for the next generation.
